Stirling engines

Stirling engine is a heat engine that is operated by a cyclic compression and expansion of air or other gas at different temperatures, resulting in a net conversion of heat energy to mechanical work. More specifically, the Stirling engine is a closed-cycle regenerative heat engine with a permanently gaseous working fluid. Closed-cycle, in this context, means a thermodynamic system in which the working fluid is permanently contained within the system.
 1-The Stirling engine is made of main parts
-Power piston and Displacer piston
-Gas
-Fly-wheel         

-Fly-wheel Power piston and displacer piston move according to the expansion and contraction of gas in the chamber as a result of the continues change of the temperature of the gas best described by Amontons law which states that that the pressure of a given mass of gas varies directly with the absolute temperature of the gas, when the volume is kept constant  =k
That continues air expansion and contraction is what leads to mechanical work that the fly-wheel harvest through a rod connected to the power piston and displacer piston.
SECC uses three custom build Stirling engines with modifications for enhanced efficiency inspired from the original Stirling engine beta design. Each Stirling engine chamber is independent of the other except for a small gap between each Stirling engine to stabilize the pressure between them so that they would all work in sync relatively to each other. SECC uses Helium instead of air as a gas due to air having 0.26 W/MK thermal conductivity (at 300 K or 26.6 C) meanwhile Helium has 0.151 W/MK thermal conductivity (at 300 K or 26.6 C).
Helium has 580% better conductivity than air; so the temperature will get transmitted faster; so more expansions and contractions in a minute; so the power piston and displacer piston would move more; so more mechanical power output.  

Parabolic heater and heat sink

Parabolic heater otherwise known as solar collector is a parabolic arc which would be facing east. This parabolic heater aims to collect as much as possible of sunlight and focus it in one point using this equation. 


  this will reflect all the light onto a glassy pipe which will contain used car oil (synthetic oil) that will Absorb heat and Transfer it to The Stirling engine Through the pipe’s end which would be made from iron and would be put in a way that doesn’t make contact with the Stirling engine but the helium only, saving the SECC from overheating .  this parabolic heater covers an area of 2-meter square. Heat sink is the opposite of parabolic heater; it is passive heat exchanger that transfers the heat generated by a mechanical device to a fluid medium, which is atmospheric air, where it is dissipated away from the device, thereby allowing regulation of the Helium temperature.  

Compiler

  Compiler’s Main job is to collect the mechanical work from the three fly-wheel into one without a drop in efficacy.  We achieved this by using a cambelt to connect the three fly-wheels. This was inspired by how a car engine  works. This cambelt will spin with a layer of copper wires attached to it nearby magnets that surrounds the cambelt without touching it. The movement of this magnets will create a changing magnetic wave which will collide with the copper coil creating a current according to Lenz’s law.   Calculating the efficiency of SECC requires knowing each part final efficiency. Which we concluded after a lot of research. According to the article named “Modeling and simulation to determine the thermal efficiency of a parabolic solar trough collector system” the Parabolic heater has an efficiency of 80%, Abuelyamen Research paper. A beta Stirling engine efficiency is about 7.5% out of the input energy that can be increased up to 15% using helium as Karabulut research paper states.

The compiler efficiency on average is around 90% according Richard Wies that covers this in his reseach paper titled “Energy-Efficient Standalone Fossil-Fuel Based Hybrid Power Systems Employing Renewable Energy Sources” According to a Nasa research sun light’s over a meter square has around 1000 watt of energy produced in an hour, and due to our project’s parabolic heater covering over 2 meters square that would be an input of 2000 watt per hour.   With this we can calculate the outcome this the efficiency The Parabolic heater will only convert 80% which equals 1600 watt.

a chamber could make 15% of the 533 which is 80 watt per chamber. With 3 chambers that 240 watt.

Once the iron end of the glass pipe and the oil heat up to their maximum capacity, they would have stored heat, which would result in every chamber having an income of 1600 watt. That would result in 688 watt per hour. That could be achieved after an hour and a half approximately.
